12 Essential Tools for Protecting Your Data and Personal Information

As someone who values online security, I'm excited to share with you the 12 tools I use daily to protect my data, personal information, and overall privacy.

Password Manager

A password manager is a must-have tool for anyone using the internet. It helps create unique and secure passwords and removes online login friction by auto-filling strong passwords across devices.

Alternatives:

  • 1Password
  • ProtonPass
  • Bitwarden

Two-Factor Authentication

Two-factor authentication is a crucial security measure. I use a YubiKey security key and have a backup in case of an emergency.

Browser for Personal Data Usage

I use the Brave browser to track my personal data usage and avoid the data collection issues associated with the Chrome browser.

Secure Email Inbox

I use ProtonMail for secure email, especially for sensitive information and communication with my accountant, lawyer, and other trusted individuals.

Virtual Address and Phone Number

I use PostGan Mail for my virtual address and Hushed for my virtual phone number, which helps me avoid giving out my personal cell phone number.

Credit Card Protection

I use Privacy.com for my credit cards, which allows me to place time and money limits on merchant-specific cards.

Data Protection Services

I use the Legal Removal Request to scan the internet for personal information and remove it, and a credit monitoring service to track my credit and identity.
