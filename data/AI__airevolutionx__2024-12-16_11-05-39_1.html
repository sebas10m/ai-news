AI and Robotics Updates

Robotics Advancements

A new AI-powered robotic system has been developed by Engine AI to create robots that can perform tasks like household chores, surgery, and even flying cars.

Humanoid Robots

Engine AI's SE01 is a fully sized humanoid robot designed with natural human-like movement, using an end-to-end neural network solution to walk, and is said to be efficient and reliable.

Unitree G1 is a two-legged humanoid robot with advanced tech, including move at a speed of 2 meters per second and has three-finger force control hands.

UBTECH Robotics showed off some of their robots that are currently deployed on automotive production lines, which can do real work and are a big step forward for the large-scale application of humanoid robots in manufacturing.

Scientists at Perceptive developed a robot that can perform fully autonomous dental surgery, with 90% accuracy and potentially changing the world of healthcare.

Physical Intelligence developed Pi Zero, a robot brain that can handle a wide range of tasks, from folding laundry to delivering surgeries, all thanks to a combination of vision, language, and memory.

Xpeng's new humanoid robot, Iron, is actively involved in assembling Xpeng's upcoming electric vehicle, the P7 Plus, in their factories.

The Turing chip can handle large AI models with up to 30 billion parameters, making Iron more like a human in terms of flexibility and decision-making.

Xpeng's plans for flying cars, to be launched in 2026, will allow people to fly in a car that can carry six passengers up to 500 kilometers, with a top speed of 360 kilometers per hour, and cost around $279,000.
