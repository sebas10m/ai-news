VPN Protocol Comparison: OpenVPN, WireGuard, and Lightway

The VPN protocol landscape has been revolutionized with the introduction of WireGuard, which has become the default protocol for many VPNs. ExpressVPN, however, has introduced its own proprietary protocol called Lightway, claiming it to be the newest and better option. But which protocol is the best?

OpenVPN: The Time-Tested Connection Protocol

OpenVPN has been the gold standard for VPN protocols for a long time. It offers a balance of encryption and speed that has been time-tested to meet the needs of both companies and individuals. While it may not be the most efficient protocol, its reliability and stability make it a trusted choice.

WireGuard: The Efficient Open-Source Protocol

WireGuard is an open-source protocol that has gained popularity due to its efficient codebase and faster implementation speeds. It is also more stable and secure, with a smaller attack surface compared to OpenVPN. However, its lack of transparency in terms of codebase and security measures makes it harder to trust.

Lightway: The Proprietary Protocol from ExpressVPN

Lightway is ExpressVPN's proprietary protocol, which claims to offer improved speeds and efficiency. However, its closed-source nature and lack of transparency make it difficult to trust its security measures. While it may be efficient, its proprietary status raises concerns about its long-term viability and maintainability.

The Verdict: Speed and Trust

When it comes to choosing a VPN protocol, speed and trust are the most critical factors. While WireGuard and Lightway promise faster speeds and improved efficiency, their lack of transparency and security measures raises concerns. In contrast, OpenVPN's time-tested reliability and stability make it a trusted choice, but its efficiency may not be the best.

Ultimately, the choice between these protocols depends on your needs and preferences. If you value speed and efficiency, WireGuard or Lightway may be the better choice. If you prioritize trust and reliability, OpenVPN may be the way to go.

Conclusion

The VPN protocol landscape is evolving, and it's exciting to see new innovations in the industry. However, when it comes to choosing a protocol, it's essential to weigh the trade-offs between speed, efficiency, and trust. As the industry continues to shift towards more transparent and secure protocols, we can expect to see more exciting developments in the future.
