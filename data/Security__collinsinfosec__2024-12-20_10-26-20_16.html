Security X Certification: A Next-Step Option

The Security X certification is a rebranded version of the Advanced Security Practitioner (CASP) certification offered by CompTIA. This new certification aims to provide mid-level professionals with hands-on performance-based skills to advance their career in security.

Changes and Updates

  • Shift from multiple-choice questions to performance-based, hands-on certification
  • Focus on technical experts, mid-level professionals seeking to advance their skills
  • Four domains: GRC, Security Architecture, Engineering, and Operations
  • _updated content, including management techniques, zero-trust security architecture, and automation in security operations

Who is this certification for?

The Security X certification is suitable for professionals lacking hands-on experience from enterprise environments, as well as those seeking to bridge the gap between theory and practicality.

Recommendations

  • Pursue the Security Plus certification first, then move on to Security X
  • Consider the certification if your company requires it, or if you want to advance your skills
  • Observe the certification's view in reviews and content, as it's a new rebranded certification

Limitations and Concerns

The certification may have limitations, such as industry recruiters not immediately recognizing it, and potential decommissioning in three years.

This article provides an overview of the Security X certification, its updates, and who it's suitable for. It's essential to consider the certification's limitations and potential impact on your career.

Final Thoughts

The Security X certification offers a next-step option for professionals seeking to advance their skills in security. However, it's crucial to evaluate its limitations and potential impact on your career before pursuing it.
