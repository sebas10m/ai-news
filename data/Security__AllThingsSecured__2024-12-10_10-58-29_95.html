Setting Up Google Authenticator for Two-Factor Authentication

Imagine being able to secure your online accounts with an extra layer of protection. Welcome to All Things Secured, where we walk you through simple steps to increase your security and privacy. Today, we'll show you how to set up Google Authenticator on your mobile devices for two-factor authentication.

Getting Started

To begin, you'll need to download and install the Google Authenticator app from the App Store on your iPad, iPhone, or Android device.

Configuring Two-Factor Authentication

  1. Open the Google Authenticator app and tap "Begin Setup."
  2. Choose to either scan a barcode or manually enter a code, and then select an account to link (in this example, we're using Facebook).
  3. Go to your account's Settings and Privacy, then click on Settings and navigate to Security and Login.
  4. Scroll down to Two-Factor Authentication and click Edit.
  5. Select "Use Authentication App" and scan the QR code with your mobile device.

Once you've set up two-factor authentication, you'll receive a six-digit code that refreshes every 20 seconds. This code can be used for logging into your account, and it poses no security risk as it changes frequently.

Logging In with Two-Factor Authentication

  1. Log into your account by entering your password and username.
  2. Google Authenticator will prompt you for a second level of authentication.
  3. Open the Google Authenticator app and select the corresponding account, then enter the six-digit code.

With Google Authenticator, you can securely log into multiple online accounts, such as Facebook, Twitter, or investment accounts, using two-factor authentication.
