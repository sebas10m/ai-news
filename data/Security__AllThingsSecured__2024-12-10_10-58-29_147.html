Switching to ProtonPass: A Decision Years in the Making

After years of using 1Password, I've finally switched to ProtonPass. In this article, I'll share why I made the switch, what features convinced me to do so, and what features I still miss from 1Password.

Why the Switch?

ProtonPass's focus on privacy and security, along with its steady stream of improvements, finally got my attention. Proton's reputation for secure communication through ProtonMail, and its feature-rich password manager, convinced me to make the switch.

ProtonPass's Features

  • Open-source code: ProtonPass offers open-source code, giving users more control over their data.
  • Free tier: ProtonPass offers a free tier with unlimited logins and devices, a rare feature in the password manager market.
  • Email aliasing: ProtonPass's email aliasing feature provides better security and control over email accounts.
  • Shared vaults: ProtonPass introduces shared vaults, allowing users to share passwords with family or coworkers.
  • Secure link: ProtonPass offers an encrypted link feature, allowing users to share specific logins without compromising security.
  • Extra password feature: ProtonPass allows users to create a separate password for their vault, adding an extra layer of security.
  • Biometrics and unique pin: ProtonPass offers biometric login and a unique pin option for added security.
  • Cross-platform compatibility: ProtonPass has dedicated apps for iOS, Android, Windows, MacOS, and Linux.
  • Credit card info storage and autofill: ProtonPass allows users to store and autofill credit card information, making it more versatile as a secure information manager.

What I Still Miss from 1Password

  • Auto login/auto submit: ProtonPass does not offer the auto login/auto submit feature, which I relied on in 1Password.
  • File attachments: During the import process, ProtonPass does not import file attachments, which I relied on in 1Password.
  • Passkeys: Unfortunately, I was not able to export my passkeys from 1Password into ProtonPass, highlighting a limitation in passkeys.

In conclusion, ProtonPass's features, both old and new, have convinced me to switch from 1Password. Its focus on privacy, security layers, email aliasing, and seamless cross-platform experience make it an attractive option for password management.
