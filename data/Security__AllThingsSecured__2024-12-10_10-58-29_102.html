Preparing Your Digital Life for Death: A Three-Part Process

Step 1: Identify and Organize Your Digital Assets

This includes your email account, banking, social media accounts, and any crypto wallets. Also, include cloud storage accounts, logins for your home utilities, your Amazon account, your PayPal account, and any other recurring billing accounts.

Step 2: Secure Your Digital Assets

Store your password vault, 2FA backup codes, and digital copies of your most important documents in a secure location. Use a password manager, such as 1Password, to store all your passwords. Consider using a secure hardware USB key or a safe deposit box at your bank.

How to Prepare Your Digital Death File:

  1. Make sure it's offline and not stored in a cloud service like Dropbox or Google Drive.
  2. Make it accessible to your loved ones or trustees.
  3. Consider distributing it, especially if you have high-value assets.

Step 3: Communicate Your Digital Death File to Your Loved Ones

Sit down with your spouse or kids and walk them through the file and its contents. Write out instructions for using the documents and store them away with your will. Consider setting up a legacy contact for your social media accounts, but this method is simpler and more reliable.
