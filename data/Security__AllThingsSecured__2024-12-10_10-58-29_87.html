Virtual Address Services: A Guide to Securing Your Mail

If you're looking for a way to create better privacy around your home or business address, or you want to simply digitize your physical mail, it's a lot easier than you might think.

Best Virtual Address Services

  • Traveling Mailbox

    Traveling Mailbox offers 2FA security for login, secure facility, and connects with Evernote and Dropbox. Their plans are reasonable, and they have a North Carolina address for speedy service.

  • UK Postbox

    UK Postbox has pay-as-you-go options, free delivery, and great business options for those doing business in the UK. However, the price per letter received is a bit high.

  • Post Scan Mail

    Post Scan Mail offers international dresses and local pickup. They have addresses in the US, the UK, Canada, the Czech Republic, and Switzerland. However, they don't have fax or check deposit features.

  • iPostale

    iPostale has over 2000 addresses, locations in the US, Europe, and China, and is one of the cheapest options. However, their phone fax features are only available with virtual office plans.

For those who have never set up a virtual address before, here are the simple four steps to follow:

  1. Choose your address and create an account with the service that best fits your needs.
  2. Complete any necessary government requirements, such as a notarized USPS form 1583.
  3. Download the app for the company you decide to use and set up notifications.
  4. Slowly start changing the address that you're giving out for personal and business purposes.

Trust me when I say that it's worth the switch. It's really not that expensive and it helps you control your physical mail, particularly if you're living overseas, but definitely if you want more privacy for your home or business address.
