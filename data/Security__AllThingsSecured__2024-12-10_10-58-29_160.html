CyberGhost VPN Review: A Solid VPN with Some Missing Features

CyberGhost is a solid piece of software that offers some great features and benefits, but it also has some missing features and questions about its company ownership.

Missing Features

  • Lack of features for Apple devices, such as a VPN kill switch, split tunneling, and the OpenVPN protocol.
  • Need to register every device that you want to use with the service, unlike most VPNs that have a simultaneous connection.

Company Ownership

The company's ownership is a concern, with a history of being sold to an Israeli company known as Crossrider, which was involved in hiding malware in software bundles and infecting user computers with adware.

Performance

In the tests that I performed, the software was effective in hiding my IP address, preventing any leaks, granting me access to geo-blocked services like Netflix, Disney+, Hulu, and others, and giving me generally fast speeds.

Conclusion

CyberGhost is a good option for those looking for a cheaper alternative with great smart DNS options and easy-to-use apps, but it's essential to understand the missing features and concerns about its company ownership before signing up.
