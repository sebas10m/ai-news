Create a Pseudonym to Protect Your Privacy

Meet Jason, a pseudonym I use to protect my identity online. As someone who values privacy, I decided to take a step further by creating a pseudonym. But before you think I'm taking extreme measures, consider this: have you ever been asked for your phone number or email address and didn't want to give it out?

Pseudonyms are not just for famous authors or spies. With the internet monitoring our every move, a pseudonym can be a useful tool to hide your data from unwanted advertisers and scammers.

What is a Pseudonym?

A pseudonym is a fake identity that can be a part of a community with a backstory, photo, and address. But for everyday people, it's simply a means of cloaking or hiding your data from those who don't need it.

Be clear: pseudonyms are not for evading government authorities. That's a different story.

Why Do I Need a Pseudonym?

Think about it: when someone asks for your phone number at a bar, or a store wants your loyalty program details, do you really want to give them your real information? A pseudonym can be a buffer between your real identity and those who don't need to know.

How Do I Create a Pseudonym?

There are two primary ways to create a pseudonym: manual or automated. The manual process involves creating a random name, registering a new email account, setting up a virtual card, and buying a second phone number. Alternatively, you can use apps like IronVest, MySudo, Cloaked, or SurfJark to create and organize your pseudonyms.

Tools to Help You Cloak Your Data

  • Dilemma: A service that helps you search for and remove your personal data from the internet.
  • Alternative ID: A platform that allows you to create and manage different pseudonyms for various purposes.

Remember, creating a pseudonym is not about hiding from authorities, but about protecting your data from unwanted contacts.

Conclusion

Cloaking your email, phone number, and credit card information is easier than you think and provides legitimate protection online. Start by thinking about erasing some of your current data from the internet and then consider creating a pseudonym to hide your contact data.
