Do You Really Need Identity Theft Protection?

Most people don't need identity theft protection, especially if they're not at high risk, willing to monitor their own data, and don't mind the inconvenience of a credit freeze.

The Problem with Online Security and Identity Theft

Online security and identity theft services often prey on our emotions of fear and worry, using marketing tactics to motivate us to take action.

What Do Identity and Credit Monitoring Services Provide?

  • Security monitoring of various information that could be harmful to you, including change of address monitoring, court records, credit reports, the dark web, sex offender registries, and social media.
  • Alerting services in case your data is found in a place it shouldn't be.
  • Recovery services in the event of identity or credit theft, including up to $1 million in insurance policy.

Who Would Benefit from Using Identity and Credit Monitoring Services?

People who are at high risk, haven't had their identity stolen in the past, and are willing to self-monitor don't need these services. However, those who are lazy, lack awareness of the benefits of identity monitoring, or have a lot to lose may benefit from using these services.

DIY Methods for Preventing Identity Theft

  • Requesting a free credit report from the three major credit bureaus once a year.
  • Placing a credit freeze with each credit bureau to prevent credit fraud.
  • Using a password manager to secure your passwords and prevent identity theft.

Remember, these services are not necessary for most people, and DIY methods can be effective in preventing identity theft.

Conclusion

Identity and credit monitoring services are not a one-size-fits-all solution. Most people can do without them, especially if they're willing to monitor their own data and credit. However, those who are at high risk or lack awareness of the benefits of identity monitoring may benefit from using these services.
