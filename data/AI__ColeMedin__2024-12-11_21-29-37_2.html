Partnership Between Automator and StackBlitz Announced

A partnership between Automator and StackBlitz has been announced, with Autodev, the open source AI coding assistant, set to become the official open source version of Bolt.new and renaming to Bolt.diy.

Discussion with Eric

Eric, CEO of StackBlitz, was interviewed to discuss the partnership and its implications.

  • Eric praised the community behind Autodev and the innovation that had occurred with the addition of LLMs.
  • He noted that the partnership would enable the testing of AI models against a reliable target, providing a real-world perspective on how well these models perform.
  • Eric highlighted the importance of educational projects, such as Autodev, in teaching people how to work with AI coding assistants and filling a gap in the current landscape.

Future Direction of Bolt.diy

The creator of Autodev outlined the future direction of Bolt.diy, focusing on educational projects, cutting-edge features, and collaboration with the community.

  • Bolt.diy will remain an open source platform, separate from the commercial Bolt.new.
  • The project will have creative freedom to implement features and manage the community as desired.
  • The partnership with StackBlitz will enable events, expert involvement, and other collaborations to drive the project forward.

Transition and Next Steps

The creator emphasized the importance of being on the same page regarding the partnership and transition, highlighting key points:

  • The Automator think tank will remain the home of the Bolt.diy community.
  • The repository will move to the StackBlitz lab organization in GitHub, but the repo itself will remain the same.
  • Autodev will maintain creative freedom, with commercial Bolt.new diverging from Bolt.diy.
  • The name change to Bolt.diy is approved, with DIY standing for "do it yourself."

Further announcements and developments will follow, with the creator excited for the future of Bolt.diy and the partnership with StackBlitz.
