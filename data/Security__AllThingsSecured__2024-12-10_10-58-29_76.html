7 Absolutely Free Tools to Prevent Online Identity Theft and Hacking

Identity theft and hacking are serious concerns in today's digital age. However, preventing these threats doesn't have to break the bank. There are several free tools available that can help protect your online identity.

Tool Number One: Bitwarden

Bitwarden is a free, open-source password manager app that makes it easy to create strong passwords and store them securely.

Tool Number Two: Free Credit Report Check

Each of the major credit bureaus (Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion) offers a free credit report check once a year. Checking your credit report regularly can help you identify suspicious activity and prevent identity theft.

Tool Number Three: Signal or Telegram

Signal and Telegram offer zero-knowledge, end-to-end encryption, making them a secure way to communicate with others.

Tool Number Four: Two-Factor Authentication

Two-factor authentication (2FA) adds an extra layer of security to your online accounts by requiring a second form of verification in addition to your password.

Tool Number Six: Redirect Detective

Redirect Detective is a free tool that allows you to check where shortened URLs lead to, helping you avoid malicious websites and data theft.

Tool Number Seven: Automatic Updates

Keeping your operating system, programs, and apps up-to-date with the latest security patches is essential for preventing hacking and identity theft.

Remember, online security is a top priority. By using these free tools, you can significantly reduce your risk of identity theft and hacking.
