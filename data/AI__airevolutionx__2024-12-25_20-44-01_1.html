Genesis: A Game-Changing Simulation Platform for Robotics and AI

Genesis is an open-source platform that's not just a physics engine but a complete simulation ecosystem. It's built to solve problems that have held back robotics research for years.

Speed and Efficiency

Genesis runs simulations up to 430,000 times faster than real life, clocking 43 million frames per second on an RTX 4090. This means robots can train in hours instead of decades.

Robust Physics Engine

At the core of Genesis is a robust physics engine designed to handle a wide range of materials and phenomena. It integrates state-of-the-art solvers for rigid bodies, fluids, and even soft bodies into one unified framework.

Generative Capabilities

Genesis can create entire 3D worlds based on natural language. Typing a simple description generates interactive scenes, task proposals, character movements, and more.

Compatibility and Accessibility

Genesis is built natively in Python, making it more accessible and user-friendly than platforms relying on C++ or CUDA for their core calculations. It works across Linux, MacOS, and Windows, and supports CPUs, NVIDIA, AMD, and Apple Metal back-ends.

Future of Simulation

Researchers are developing 4D dynamic worlds, simulated environments that are not only 3-dimensional but also incorporate motion over time. These environments are created using vision language models, allowing users to describe scenarios in natural language, which Genesis then brings to life.

Scalability

Genesis can run up to 100,000 copies of a simulation at the same time. This parallelized simulation capability is invaluable for training neural networks.

Open-Source and Community-Driven

Genesis is fully open source, meaning anyone can contribute to its development or customize it for their needs. The team encourages collaboration and feedback through GitHub, where users can suggest features, report bugs, and share ideas.
