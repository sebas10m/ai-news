Disruption and Monopoly

Every company gets fat, dumb, and happy to some degree.

Companies can either do a better job and improve their capabilities or get disrupted by new technologies and ideas.

A monopoly is not necessarily evil, but it can be anticompetitive and stifle innovation.

The question is whether a company is providing a fantastic product and service, or if it is engaging in monopolistic behavior.

Antitrust regulation aims to promote competition and create opportunities for others to come in and disrupt.

Every company gets disrupted eventually, and new technologies constantly emerge.

Crazy ideas can become breakthroughs, but companies may be unwilling to take risks.

Freedom and competition are essential for innovation, but there are limits to the market's ability to provide certain goods and services.

For some problems, collective public goods and social innovation are needed to create a fair society.
