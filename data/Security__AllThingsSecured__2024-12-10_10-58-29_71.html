Protecting Yourself Online

In today's digital age, it's essential to take steps towards securing your data and identity online. Here are five simple things you can do to vastly increase your security and privacy:

Choose a Great Password

A great password uses capital letters, numbers, symbols, and at least eight characters. You can create a strong password by using a search engine and modifying a phrase or song. For example, you can take "itsy bitsy spider" and replace the "I" with an exclamation point and the "S" with the number 5.

Keep Your Software and Apps Up to Date

Regularly updating your software and apps will help patch up security weaknesses and protect you from known security issues. Don't ignore the prompts from Microsoft, Google, and Apple to update your software and apps.

Use a VPN

A Virtual Private Network (VPN) is a useful tool for creating security and protecting your online activity. Use a VPN when accessing public Wi-Fi or sensitive information online.

Secure Your Social Media

Control who can see your social media posts by adjusting your privacy settings on platforms like Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter. Be mindful of what information you share online and avoid sharing sensitive data unnecessarily.

Monitor Your Bank Accounts

Regularly review your bank accounts to spot errors and fraudulent charges. Set up automatic bill payments, and reconcile your accounts at the end of each month to ensure accuracy and security.

Use Common Sense

Take small but significant steps to protect yourself online, such as setting up passwords for your devices, enabling two-factor authentication, and avoiding unnecessary sharing of sensitive information. Your security and privacy online depend on you, so take responsibility and make improvements today.
