Strategies for Digital Anonymity

Completely disappearing from the internet has become virtually impossible, but certain habits and tools can make it more challenging to track your online activities.

Method One: Minimize Personal Data Exposure

When providing identification, use a passport instead of a driver's license, as it contains less personal information.

Method Two: Virtual Address and Masked Phone Number

  • Use a virtual address for online transactions, which receives mail and packages and sends notifications to your phone.
  • Mask your personal phone number using services like Hushed or Google Voice.

Method Three: Secure Email and Online Transactions

Utilize services like Hide My Email, Fastmail, or Blur by Abine to mask your email address and create secure online transactions.

Method Four: Virtual Credit Cards

Create virtual credit cards for online transactions, allowing you to change or cancel the card if necessary.

Method Five: Data Scrubbing and Social Media Caution

  • Use a service like Delete Me to scrub your personal data from the internet.
  • This includes ensuring social media accounts are private and sensitive information is not publicly posted.

Method Six: Browser Privacy Settings

Utilize browsers like Firefox or Brave to configure privacy settings and block tracking cookies and scripts.

Method Seven: Virtual Private Networks (VPNs) and Tor Connections

Use VPNs or Tor connections to mask your IP address and location data while browsing online.
