Cyber Security Certificates Tier List

Part 1: Old School

CompTIA Security Plus

CompTIA Security Plus is a good introduction to cyber security, introducing important general information about cyber security. However, it's not a great way to learn a subject, and its multiple-choice exam format can lead to memorization rather than practical skills. The price is also a drawback, at $450 for the exam alone.

Rating: C

Cisco Certificates

Cisco certifications, such as CCNA and CCNP, are not necessary for a cybersecurity career. However, their training courses, such as the Cisco Cyber Security Analyst, are of low quality. The price is free, but you get what you pay for.

Rating: D

Part 2: Second Generation

Offensive Security OSCP

The OSCP is a practical, hands-on training that mimics real-world penetration testing. However, its training course is not properly structured, and the organization's recent acquisition has led to price increases and the introduction of unnecessary training.

Rating: A (formerly S)

Part 3: New Generation

Let's Defend, Cyber Defenders, Try Hack Me, Hack The Box, and Blue Team Level One and Two

These courses provide practical, hands-on training that gives you skills used on the job. They are reasonably priced and essential for a cybersecurity career. However, individuals often get confused about which platform to choose.

Rating: S-Tier

Google Cyber Security Cert

This certificate provides a basic, broad introduction to cybersecurity and is essential for those new to the field.

Rating: S-Tier

Part 4: Best and Worst

Cyber Security Boot Camps

Cyber security boot camps promise job placement but are scams, charging thousands of dollars for subpar training. They use shady sales tactics to pressure customers into buying.

Rating: F-Tier
