Automated Reasoning Revolution

Automated reasoning is a field of computer science that has long been a challenge for even the largest companies in the world. However, recent advancements in AI may havefinally solved this problem.

The key issue with automated reasoning is the cost of getting it wrong. A single error can have catastrophic consequences, making it a mission-critical task.

What is Automated Reasoning?

Automated reasoning is the science of using mathematical logic to prove the validity of statements.

Imagine a hospital having a policy for treating patients, or an airline having a refund policy. Automated reasoning needs to be perfect, with no ambiguity, as it has to be proven with math.

Why is Automated Reasoning so Difficult?

Airline refund policies, for instance, are complex and have numerous rules and situations. If there's even one loophole, it can be exploited, leading to catastrophic consequences.

AWS's IAM permissioning system is another example, where the cost of giving someone the wrong set of permissions is catastrophic.

How is Automated Reasoning Currently Solved?

Currently, automated reasoning is solved by writing complex code using mathematical logic. This requires a team of logicians and computer scientists working together for years, costing millions of dollars.

What's New with Amazon Bedrock Guardrails Automated Reasoning Checks?

AWS has solved the problem of hallucinations in AI-generated code. Their automated reasoning system can load a natural language document, extract logical rules, and convert it to a logical language that can be proven with 100% certainty.

The system can test itself and provide explanations for its answers, ensuring the truthfulness of large language models.

Contextual Grounding vs. Automated Reasoning

Contextual grounding, although successful, is not explainable. Automated reasoning, on the other hand, provides transparent and explainable reasoning for the truthfulness of statements.

AWS's automated reasoning checks in Amazon Bedrock guardrails is the sixth type of safeguard being launched into their guardrails product, and it's a game-changer.
