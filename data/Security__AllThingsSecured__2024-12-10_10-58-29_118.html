Email Aliases: Setting Up a System to Work for You

Email aliases are a simple yet effective way to categorize and anonymize your emails, providing stronger security for your digital life.

Understanding Email Aliases

An email alias is an email address created for a specific purpose, different from an email account or inbox.

For the purpose of this video, the word "inbox" refers to a specific account where emails are received and stored, while "email address" refers to a unique email alias.

My Email Alias Setup

Personal Account

I have a personal account that I don't give out to anyone except family and close friends.

Business Account

I have a business account for all business communication, with separate email aliases for payments, advertising, and LinkedIn.

Encrypted ProtonMail Inbox

I use this account for secure communication and financial account logins, providing end-to-end encryption and private communication.

Alias Account (Jason)

I use this account for occasions where I need to give out a name and email address without sharing my real name and email.

Email Alias Services

Mentioned services include Anon Addy, Hide My Email, MailDrop, and Simple Login, which create unique aliases for every login and provide a triage for email management.

Using Alias Groups

I use groups for newsletters, social media accounts, content streaming services, shopping accounts, and general marketing materials, each with its own alias and filters to keep my primary inbox clear.

Conclusion

Email aliases are an essential tool for categorizing and anonymizing your emails, providing stronger security and organization.

By implementing a system of email aliases, you can reduce spam, increase security, and improve your digital life.
