All Things Secured - Protecting Your Identity Online

I'm Josh, and I'm a big proponent of using security software and hardware to protect your accounts and identity online. However, one habit that can make a significant difference in preventing scams is verifying elsewhere.

A Common Scam

This scam, where a person asks for help with an urgent issue by claiming to be someone you trust, is undetectable by antivirus or VPN software. The scammer may even ask you to do a task for them, such as sending a check.

What to Do

Instead of relying on pressure from the scammer, verify the request elsewhere. This means making a phone call, sending a message on a different platform, or even making a video call like FaceTime or Signal.

Why Verify Elsewhere?

Verifying elsewhere is crucial because scams often involve urgent situations that create pressure to act quickly. But by taking a step back and verifying the request, you can avoid losing money or falling victim to a scam.

A Real-Life Example

A business email compromise scam targeted Shark Tank investor Barbara Corcoran's firm, attempting to scam them out of $400,000. However, they were able to avoid the scam by verifying elsewhere, which highlights the importance of this habit.

Conclusion

Becoming proficient in verifying elsewhere is a crucial step in protecting yourself and your loved ones from scams. Take a few tips from the video to create this habit in your circle of friends, and remember, you always have time to verify.
