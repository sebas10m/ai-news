Understanding Session Hijacking and Account Takeover

In this video, a hacker demonstrates how to steal and take over any account on any website on the internet, using a safe and controlled demo environment. The hacker starts by accessing a Reddit account using a strong and complex password, as well as two-factor authentication.

The hacker notes that session hijacking is a threat in the cybersecurity landscape that can lead to full account takeovers, data breaches, and financial loss. They explain that session hijacking relies on cookies used to communicate with the website, and that it can be obnoxiously easy to do.

Exporting Cookies with the Extension

The hacker uses a browser extension to export the cookies from the Reddit account, and then views the exported cookies in a text file. They note that cookies can be represented in different formats, and that the Netscape format is commonly used by InfoStealer malware.

Extracting Information from InfoStealer Logs

The hacker extracts information from the InfoStealer logs, including passwords, autofill information, and cookies. They note that the red line variant of InfoStealer malware had been super common in the past.

Hacking the Reddit Account

The hacker uses the Hoax Shell utility to generate a payload andReverse shell to hack the Reddit account. They create a PowerShell script to scrape Firefox cookies in the Netscape format and use ngrok to stage and serve the payload to the victim.

Using an Anti-Detect Browser to Log in to Reddit

The hacker uses an anti-detect browser to log in to the Reddit account without knowing the password or multi-factor authentication. They create a new account with minute inbox dot com and use the anti-detect browser to configure the browser settings to match the victim's settings.

Preventing Session Hijacking with Flair

The hacker discusses the importance of having visibility and threat intelligence to defend against session hijacking. They showcase the Flair API's ability to monitor leaked cookies and invalidate them before they are taken over and used for session hijacking or account takeover.

Conclusion

The hacker concludes that session hijacking and account takeover can be a problem even with complex and strong passwords and multi-factor authentication. They emphasize the importance of having visibility and threat intelligence to defend against these threats, and thanks Flair for sponsoring the video.
