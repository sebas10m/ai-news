Hiding Your Home Address for Privacy

For the sake of your home privacy, I recommend that everybody completes at least the first two steps I'm going to show you. The third step is reserved only for those who are absolutely paranoid about their privacy.

Step One: The Reset

We need to take a hard look at how your home address is currently being used, both by you and by others. Check sites like White Pages, Spokio, Family Tree Now, Google, and others to see where your address might be available.

Change your address on social media, with Google, or even your bank to keep your home address private.

Step Two: Acquire a New, Somewhat Anonymous Address

A P.O. Box may not be the best option since it limits what kind of mail you can receive. Instead, consider using a virtual mailbox service like US Global Mail, iPostal One, or Traveling Mailbox.

Virtual mailboxes digitize your physical mail while keeping your real home address private. You can set it up under your personal name or company name for extra privacy.

Step Three (Optional): Setting Up a Privacy Trust

This step requires a significant monetary investment and a dedication to privacy. Consider consulting a legal expert to help you set up a privacy trust that will hold most of your assets from your personal residence to your car or any other vehicle.

This strategy is often used by celebrities and wealthy people to hide the location of their personal residence or assets.

Ultimately, the best thing you can do is to get to a place where you never write down or give out your actual home address unless absolutely necessary.

Following the steps outlined here means scrubbing the internet of your current physical address and purchasing a virtual address that you can use from now on.

This is a smart idea from a privacy standpoint and a very convenient way to control your physical mail and cut down on the spam.
