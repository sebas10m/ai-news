What are PassKeys and How Do They Work?

Google's new FIDO security standard, PassKeys, allows users to sign into their accounts without a password. In this article, we'll explore the setup process and the difference between personal and paid Workspace accounts.

Setup Process

To set up a PassKey, log into your account and navigate to the security settings. You'll see the option to create a PassKey. You can choose to use Touch ID or a password to verify your identity.

Difference Between Personal and Paid Workspace Accounts

PassKeys are only available for personal Google accounts, not paid Workspace accounts. When logging into a Workspace account, you'll need to use the regular verification method involving a password and 2FA key.

Benefits of PassKeys

PassKeys make it harder for bad actors to gain access to your account. Since the PassKey only works with the website or app that created it, you no longer need to worry about whether you're visiting a genuine website or a phishing site.

Security and Encryption

Both Apple and Google claim end-to-end encryption, which means that even they can't access the PassKeys that you store with them. Additionally, apps like 1Password will soon give you an alternative place to store and back up these PassKeys.

Future of Passwords

The goal is a future without passwords, and PassKeys are quickly becoming an option for a number of online accounts already.

Stay safe and keep exploring the latest security measures!
