Learning Cybersecurity in 2025

To land a job in cybersecurity, Jon Snow needs to learn the basics of how computers work, security principles, blue teaming, and hacking. These four pillars of knowledge are the foundation for a career in cybersecurity.

The First Pillar: Basic IT and Cybersecurity Knowledge

The first pillar is to gain basic IT and cybersecurity knowledge through affordable entry-level courses. Coursera offers a subscription-based model that gives access to every course, including Google IT and Google cybersecurity courses. For a limited time, Coursera has a $200 off their Coursera Plus annual subscription, making a year's worth of access to every single course just $199.

The Second Pillar: Security Principles

The second pillar is to learn security principles through CompTIA's Security Plus. This course covers the CIA triad and gives a comprehensive understanding of security principles. Completing the Google Cybersecurity Professional Certificate earns a discount towards CompTIA's Security Plus.

The Third Pillar: Defending Networks and Endpoints

The third pillar is to learn what tools are out there to defend networks, endpoints, and websites. Try HackMe's SOC Level 1 learning path is a beginner-friendly format that introduces security tools. Completing this path prepares you for the fourth pillar.

The Fourth Pillar: Offense and Investigation

The fourth pillar is to learn how offense works and how to investigate security incidents. Try HackMe's SOC Level 2 learning path and TCM Security's PSAA course provide a deeper understanding of security analyst and SOC analyst investigation mythologies across multiple security tools.

Getting Started

Stop making excuses and get going. With dedication and hard work, Jon Snow can gain the four pillars of knowledge needed to land a job in cybersecurity. Start with Coursera and work your way up to the other courses.
