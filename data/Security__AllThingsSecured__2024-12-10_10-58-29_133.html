Migrating 2FA Codes from Google Authenticator

Before migrating your 2FA codes, it's essential to understand three key concepts:

  • 2FA codes are mathematical equations, not generated by Google, so there's no "forgot password" option.
  • Make sure to have a backup of all your 2FA codes, including the QR code and 12-digit backup code.
  • You can easily set up more than one device or authenticator app during the setup process.

Method 1: Exporting 2FA Codes using QR Code

To export your 2FA codes, follow these steps:

  1. Open the Google Authenticator app and click on the three dots in the upper right corner.
  2. Click on "Export accounts." You can only export 10 accounts at a time.
  3. Choose which codes to export and click on "Export."
  4. You will receive a massive QR code that you can scan or screenshot for later use.
  5. Dismiss the warnings and wait until you verify your codes before removing all accounts on your old device.

Method 2: Manual Method

The manual method is necessary if you want to export to a different authenticator app or feel like your codes have been compromised. Here's how to do it:

  1. Log into each individual account and find the security settings for 2FA.
  2. Turn off 2FA and then set it back up again, or add a new 2FA authenticator.
  3. You won't be able to access the original QR code, so you'll need to reset and start from scratch.

Remember to be patient and take your time when migrating your 2FA codes. If you have any questions, feel free to ask in the comments below.
