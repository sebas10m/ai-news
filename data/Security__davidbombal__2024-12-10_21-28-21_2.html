Cisco: The Networking Pioneers

Cisco, or Cisco Systems, was founded on December 10, 1984, and is now 40 years old.

Cisco is famous for its routers and switches, which have changed the world in terms of networking.

The Cisco AGS: An Early Router

This is the second generation of the Advanced Gateway Server Router (AGS), a device that was the "OG" of routers.

The AGS was used extensively in the 1980s and early 1990s, and was known for its reliability and versatility.

Connecting to the Internet

The AGS was used to connect to the internet using various technologies, including 10Base2 and 10Base5.

These older technologies are still used today in some parts of the world, particularly in areas with limited access to modern infrastructure.

A Starlink Connection

The AGS was also used to connect to the internet using a Starlink satellite dish.

This connection was established using an Ethernet cable, which was plugged into the AGS and then routed to the Starlink router.

Conclusion

This video demonstrates the ability of older networking equipment to still connect to the internet today.

The Cisco AGS, in particular, has shown to be a reliable and versatile device that can still handle modern networking protocols.
