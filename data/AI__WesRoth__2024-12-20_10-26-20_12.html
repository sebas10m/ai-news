Genesis Project

The Genesis project is a generative AI model that produces 4-dimensional dynamical worlds powered by a physics simulation platform.

Unlike traditional generative AI models, Genesis simulates the physics of our world, allowing robots to learn and perform tasks in a highly realistic and accurate way.

Key Features

  • Generative physics engine
  • Simulates 4-dimensional dynamical worlds
  • Powered by a physics simulation platform
  • 10-80 times faster than GPU-accelerated stacks like Isaac Jim
  • Can simulate a wide range of materials, including rigid bodies, cloth, liquids, and deformables
  • Supports native non-convex collision handling
  • Can create complex characters and environments for training robots

Sim2Real Policy Transfer

The sim2real policy transfer allows robots to learn in a simulated environment and then transfer that knowledge to the real world, enabling them to perform tasks with high accuracy and agility.

This technology has the potential to revolutionize the field of robotics and AI, enabling the creation of more sophisticated and autonomous robots.

Conclusion

The Genesis project is an exciting development in the field of AI and robotics, with the potential to enable the creation of more sophisticated and autonomous robots.
