How to Put a Freeze on Your Credit

Putting a freeze on your credit is an essential step in protecting your financial data from being compromised. In this tutorial, we will show you how to put a freeze on your credit with each of the three major credit bureaus: Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion.

Why Should You Put a Freeze on Your Credit?

Your credit score is a reflection of your financial responsibility and is used by lenders to determine your creditworthiness. However, your credit score can also be used by identity thieves to open credit accounts in your name. By putting a freeze on your credit, you can prevent this from happening.

What is a Credit Freeze?

A credit freeze is a request to the credit bureau to prevent them from sharing your credit information with lenders. This means that if someone tries to open a credit account in your name, the credit bureau will not share your credit information with the lender, effectively blocking the account from being opened.

How to Put a Freeze on Your Credit with Each Credit Bureau

Equifax

To put a freeze on your credit with Equifax, you will need to create an account and agree to the terms and conditions. You will then need to verify your identity by answering a series of questions and providing your social security number.

Once your identity has been verified, you will be able to add a freeze to your credit file. You will be given a PIN number that you will need to use to thaw or unfreeze your credit.

What to Do After You Have Put a Freeze on Your Credit

Once you have put a freeze on your credit, it is essential to keep your login information or PIN number safe. You will need to use this information to thaw or unfreeze your credit when you need to.

Additionally, you should consider putting a freeze on the credit of any family members, including children, to protect their financial data as well.
