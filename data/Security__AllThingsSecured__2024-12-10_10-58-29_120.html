Amazon Data Collection and Use of Personal Information

Amazon tracks a wide range of data points about its customers, including:

  • Every order placed on the website
  • Every show watched on Amazon Prime Video
  • Every song listened to on Prime Music
  • Every request made on Alexa
  • Every record of motion on Ring doorbells
  • Kindle reading data, including date, time, and highlighted passages
  • Audible account data
  • Fire TV usage data
  • Physical address
  • Credit history
  • Search history
  • Website clicks
  • Internet usage via advertisements

Amazon collects this data through three main channels:

  • The data we voluntarily provide to Amazon, such as credit card information and address
  • Metadata collected by Amazon, including device type, IP address, and browsing history
  • Data received from third parties, including credit bureaus and subsidiaries

Stopping Amazon's Data Collection

While Amazon's data collection practices have raised concerns, there are limited options for consumers to stop or limit Amazon's tracking:

  • Delete your Amazon account, which is the most extreme option
  • Use a web browser that blocks third-party cookies while shopping on Amazon
  • Use a different browser for shopping and browsing
  • Pay for Amazon purchases using a virtual credit card
  • Use a virtual address for package delivery

Conclusion

While Amazon's data collection practices are extensive, there are steps consumers can take to limit Amazon's data collection. However, these options are limited and require significant changes to daily behavior.
