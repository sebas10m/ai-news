Week in Review

Google's Gemini Announcements

Google made several announcements related to Gemini, including the release of Gemini 2.0 and Project Astra.

Gemini 2.0 Flash

Gemini 2.0 Flash is a new model that surpasses Gemini 1.5 Pro in terms of performance and features.

  • Supports multimodal input and output
  • Outperforms Gemini 1.5 Pro in various benchmarks
  • CAN natively call tools like Google Search
  • Has code execution and tool calling capabilities

Project Astra

Project Astra is a new model that can use a camera to see the world and answer questions.

  • Has vision capabilities
  • Can remember things and reason about the environment
  • Potential use cases include virtual assistants and more

Emergence AI Updates

Emergence AI announced the release of their multi-agent orchestrator and a demo of a real-world use case.

  • Can browse the web on behalf of users
  • Can automate complex and sophisticated web interactions
  • Has features such as design-time flexibility and runtime determinism

Other News

Other notable news includes the shutdown of Cruise, a company acquired by General Motors, and the release of Devin, a new AI model.

  • Cruise was a technology company focused on autonomous vehicles, but its efforts will be reassigned within General Motors
  • Devin is a new AI model that is publicly available and has features such as multimodal input and output

Quantum Computing Updates

Google released Willow, a new quantum chip that can perform certain types of calculations infinitely faster than traditional computers.

  • Has a low error rate, which is a major hurdle for quantum computing
  • Can perform standard benchmark computations in under five minutes

Meta AI Updates

Meta AI released Lama 3.3, a new text model that is widely adopted and can perform at the level of a 405 billion parameter model.

  • Is open-source and has a permissive license
  • Has a vast number of downloads and is becoming an industry standard
