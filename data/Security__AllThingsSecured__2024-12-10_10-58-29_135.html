Create a Hidden Wi-Fi Network for Enhanced Security

To add an extra layer of protection to your Wi-Fi network, you can create a hidden Wi-Fi network. This requires both the network name and password to be entered manually, rather than being visible to others.

Setting up a Hidden Wi-Fi Network on Apple Airport

  1. Open the Airport app utility to access the settings for your Apple Airport base station.
  2. Go to Wireless and select the option to create a hidden network.
  3. Click Save to save the changes.

Setting up a Hidden Wi-Fi Network on a Traditional Router

  1. Look for the router login on the back of your router (this may be a URL, username, and password).
  2. Enter the required information to log in to your router.
  3. Go to the Wireless settings and find the option to enable SSID broadcast.
  4. Uncheck the box next to enable SSID broadcast.

By disabling SSID broadcast, your Wi-Fi network will no longer be visible to others, and devices will need to enter the network name and password manually to connect. This is a simple step that can significantly enhance your home security.

Additionally, there are other advanced security measures you can take to protect your personal and home security, such as using a VPN and securing your router with strong passwords.
