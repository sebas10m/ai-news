Checking Your Free Credit Report

You can request your free credit report through annualcreditreport.com, the individual credit bureau's websites (Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion), or by mailing in a request. Make sure to only use one of these methods to avoid fraud.

Requesting Your Credit Report

When requesting your credit report on annualcreditreport.com, you will be asked to provide your first name, last name, birthday, and social security number. You can choose to request all three credit reports at once or space them out over time (such as every four months).

What to Check in Your Credit Report

  • Date
  • Score
  • balances
  • Payments
  • Credit accounts
  • Inquiries
  • Accounts
  • Installments
  • Public records

Review your credit report for any errors or discrepancies, such as unknown accounts or high balances. You can dispute any issues you find.

Identity Theft Protection

If you think your information is at high risk of being stolen, consider using a credit monitoring service, such as LifeLock. These services can alert you to potential identity theft and help you take action.

Important Reminders

  • Do not use a public computer or public Wi-Fi to access your credit report.
  • Make sure to check all household members' credit reports, not just your own.
  • Do not pay for your credit report; it is a free service.

Checking your credit report regularly can help you identify and prevent identity theft. Consider setting a reminder to check your report every four months.

Conclusion

Remember to stay safe and secure when checking your credit report. Follow these tips and enjoy peace of mind knowing your identity and finances are protected.
