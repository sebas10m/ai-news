Alternatives to VPN: Protecting Your Online Privacy and Security

Mobile Hotspot: A Simple and Effective Alternative

Using your mobile phone's personal hotspot is a straightforward way to stay safe on public networks. Most phones can act as a hotspot, connecting your computer or tablet to your encrypted cellular network. Simply enable the feature, secure it with a strong password, and you're good to go.

Tor: A Free and Private Browsing Solution

Tor is a popular alternative to VPNs, offering a high level of privacy and security without the need for a monthly subscription. It works by routing your connection through multiple nodes, making it difficult for others to track your online activity.

You can use Tor in a browser or as a standalone service. Brave is a good option, offering a balance between privacy and convenience. You can also use Tor's private window for enhanced security.

Private Relay: An Alternative on Apple Devices

Apple's Private Relay service offers a secure and private connection, using paid iCloud subscriptions. While it's not a direct replacement for VPN, it provides an additional layer of protection for those who value their online security.

Decentralized VPNs: A New Era in Online Privacy

Decentralized VPNs, or DVPNs, offer peer-to-peer VPN servers, making it harder to track your online activity. Mysterium and Sentinel are two popular options, which provide a secure and private connection without the need for a centralized VPN company.

Setting Up Your Own VPN: A Long-Term Alternative

You can set up and run your own VPN server for free, offering a long-term solution for those who value their online privacy and security. While the process is more complex than using a commercial VPN, it provides a high level of control and flexibility.

Conclusion

Alternatives to VPNs are not only more affordable but also provide a high level of online security and privacy. By using your mobile hotspot, Tor, Private Relay, or even setting up your own VPN server, you can stay safe online without breaking the bank.
