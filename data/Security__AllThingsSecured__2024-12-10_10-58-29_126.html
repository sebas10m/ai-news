How to Protect Your Privacy on Facebook

Josh here from All Things Secured. In this video, we'll cover the essential changes you need to make to your Facebook privacy settings to keep your information safe.

Limit Profile Visibility

First, look at how your profile appears to the public. If anyone can see your friends, photos, and other information, it's time to make some changes.

  1. Go to your profile page and click on the three dots at the top right corner.
  2. Click on "View As" and see how your profile looks to the public.
  3. Go to Settings and Privacy and click on "Privacy Checkup."

You can restrict visibility for your profile information by going to Settings and Privacy and clicking on Privacy Checkup.

Limit Past Posts Visibility

Click on "Limit Past Posts" and choose to limit your past posts so that only friends can see them.

Restrict Future Posts Visibility

Set who can see your future posts by default. You can choose to set it to "Friends" or "Specific Friends".

Control Profile Visibility

Decide who can send friend requests and look up your profile using your phone number and email address.

Manage Off-Facebook Activity

Facebook has a feature called Off-Facebook Activity that shows you what companies and websites are sharing your data with Facebook. You can click on "Manage Off-Facebook Activity" to see who's sharing your data and clear your history.

Set Ad Preferences

You can control what information Facebook uses to show you ads. Choose the information you don't want to be used to show you ads.

Disable Location Services

Go to your settings> devices: location> disable location services for your mobile device.

Power Tip: Use Firefox with Facebook Container

Use Firefox with Facebook Container to prevent Facebook from tracking you across the web.
