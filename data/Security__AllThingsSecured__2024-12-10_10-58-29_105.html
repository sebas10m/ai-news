Google vs DuckDuckGo: A Privacy-Conscious Consumer's Perspective

We all know that Google's business model is built around tracking our searches and customizing ads. But for a privacy-conscious consumer like yourself, it's hard to know how worrisome this really is and what power we have to fight it.

Comparing Google and DuckDuckGo

When it comes to Google services, things like YouTube, Gmail, Google Drive, and of course, the Google Search page, the biggest hurdle is the one problem that makes it so hard to move away from their services - they're simply the best. No other search engine compares to Google.

However, Google's search quality is influenced in no small part by the information it collects from you. DuckDuckGo's privacy policy guarantees that they won't have this same advantage. For example, because Google tracks your search and browsing history on the internet, they create a profile on you and serve up search results that match that profile.

Understanding DuckDuckGo's Approach

DuckDuckGo doesn't collect any personal data at all. They block tracking from Google, Facebook, and others, and their ads are based solely on the search term itself.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Google gives you the better search quality, while DuckDuckGo provides the best privacy. If privacy is your priority, it's worth giving DuckDuckGo a try.
