Methods to Change Your Perceived Location on Apple TV

While Apple TV doesn't support direct VPN app installation, there are three methods to change your perceived location on the streaming device.

Method 1: Configuring Your Wi-Fi Router

This method involves configuring your Wi-Fi router instead of your Apple TV. You can use the same VPN connection on your router by setting up a VPN configuration.

  • Log into your router by typing in the IP address.
  • Find the VPN configuration settings and download the setup files from your VPN provider.
  • Upload the.ovpn file to your router and set up the VPN connection.

Once you've set up the VPN connection, you can broadcast the connection to all devices connected to your Wi-Fi network, including your Apple TV.

Method 2: The Mirror Method

This method involves signing into streaming services through a mobile browser while connected to a VPN on your device and mirroring that onto your Apple TV.

To do this:

  • Connect to a VPN on your mobile device.
  • Sign into streaming services through a mobile browser.
  • Mirror the browser on your iPhone or iPad onto your Apple TV.

This method has its drawbacks, including lower video quality.

Method 3: Using Smart DNS

Smart DNS is a feature offered by some VPN services that only redirects your Apple TV's DNS queries, rather than encrypting all internet traffic.

However, this method has many fail points and may not work for some users. It requires registering your IP address, disabling IPv6, or contacting your internet service provider, among other things.

I've tested multiple Smart DNS services and was unable to get it to work with any of my streaming services.

Conclusion

The most reliable method to change your perceived location on Apple TV is to add a VPN to your Wi-Fi router or use the mirror method. These methods have a higher chance of success and avoid the frustration of trying to set up Smart DNS.

Remember to always explore your VPN options and test different methods to find what works best for you.
