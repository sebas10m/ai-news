Building a Data Science Team

nearly 28 minutes of valuable insights from interviewer Nestor Rothschild on building a strong data science team.

Interviewer Insights

  • For a company with an established data team, decide which project to prioritize using metrics like business impact and technical feasibility.
  • Pay attention to small things like coding standards, naming conventions, and abstraction.
  • Foresee potential issues with data quality and organization and address them before hiring.
  • Iteration, being able to learn fast, and having a good slogan would work for every company out there.

Key Takeaways

  • Establish a strong backbone of data pipelines before thinking about machine learning and hiring data scientists.
  • Hire data engineers to clean up data quality before hiring data scientists.
  • Don't work on one project; prioritize the top three based on business impact and technical feasibility.
  • When building a data science team, communicate and educate higher management about what can be accomplished.

Conclusion

This conversation provided numerous insights into building a strong data science team, particularly for companies that are just starting out. By addressing data quality, organization, and technical feasibility, you can ensure a successful journey in data science.
