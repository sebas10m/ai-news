Securing Your Freelance Business

As a freelancer or small business owner, it's essential to prioritize your security and protect your business from potential threats.

Protect Your Personal Address and Phone Number

Sharing your home address and personal phone number with clients and partners can expose you to risks. Consider using a virtual business address and virtual phone number to maintain a separation between your personal and professional life.

Manage Social Media Accounts with Care

Be cautious when sharing login details with coworkers or freelancers, and consider using two-factor authentication (2FA) to secure your social media and other online accounts.

Data Backup is Crucial

securing your data is essential, especially as a freelancer or small business owner. Setup regular backups and consider using secure cloud storage to protect your digital assets.

Test Your Business's Resilience

Imagine a scenarios where your website or social media account is compromised. Consider running regular what-if scenarios to test your business's resilience and prepare for potential threats.

Separate Personal and Business Finances

Creating a separation between your personal and business finances can provide an added layer of protection for your business. Consider using a separate bank account, credit card, and PayPal account for your business.

Protect Your Personal Data

Be cautious when sharing personal data, such as your social security number or other identifying information. Request an encrypted method to send sensitive documents or use an encrypted email service.
