All Things Secure

My name's Josh, and we're exploring the concept of always-on cameras in smartphones. This technology, developed by Qualcomm, enables the camera to constantly search for your face, even when the phone is idle. The idea is to provide convenience, but it raises questions about privacy.

How it works

Qualcomm's always-on camera uses a chip to continuously look for your face. According to Qualcomm, the camera data never leaves the secure sensing hub while it's searching for faces. This is similar to how biometric data, such as face ID and fingerprint, is stored on the phone itself and not on the manufacturer's servers.

Risks and considerations

One of the primary concerns is privacy. Even if the data stays on the chip, there's a risk that phone manufacturers, such as Huawei or Google, could access the data through backdoor access. Chinese law requires certain phone manufacturers to provide access to user data, which raises concerns about data security.

Implications and future

Currently, there are no phones that use this Snapdragon chip, but it's estimated to be available in the next year or two. Phone manufacturers will need to implement this technology, and it's up to users to decide if they're comfortable with an always-on camera.
