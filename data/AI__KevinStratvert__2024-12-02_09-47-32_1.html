Winning the Google Game: How to Use SEMrush's Content Marketing Toolset

Part 1: Examining Competitor Keywords

In this tutorial, we'll walk you through SEMrush's content marketing toolset, from A to Z. We'll cover how to generate topic ideas, structure your content, and check your articles for SEO results.

Step 1: Examine Competitor Keywords

To understand what keywords our competitors rank for, we'll use SEMrush's Organic Research tool.

We know one of our biggest competitors is Top Pot Donuts. We'll type in their URL, toppotdonuts.com, and click the search button.

On this page, we'll scroll down to the keywords section. We're looking for keywords our competitors rank for.

Filtering Keywords

We'll use SEMrush's keyword difficulty filter to exclude keywords with difficulty above 30%. We'll also exclude keywords containing the words "Top Pot" and "Donuts".

This will give us a list of easy and relevant keywords to target.

Step 2: Identify Seed Keywords

We'll identify the seed keywords to use in SEMrush's Keyword Strategy Builder.

Some of our seed keywords include: "best donuts near me," "donut holes near me," "apple fritter near me," "cocktail donuts," and "coffee donuts."

Step 3: Generate Content Strategy

We'll use the Keyword Strategy Builder to generate an entire content strategy based on our seed keywords.

This tool will suggest related keywords, pages, and sub pages to write to bring in the maximum amount of traffic.

Step 4: Write Content

We'll use SEMrush's SEO Writing Assistant to write articles that get us the traffic we want.

For this article, we'll use the "Filled Donuts near me" page as an example.

Avoiding Writer's Block

With SEMrush's tools, we can overcome writer's block and blank page syndrome. We'll use the Compose tool to write a call to action, and the Ask AI feature to get best practices for SEO.

By following these steps, we can create high-quality content that will help us win the Google game.
