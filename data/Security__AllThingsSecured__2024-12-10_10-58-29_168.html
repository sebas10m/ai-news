Mozilla VPN Review

Mozilla VPN lacks a number of features that I've come to expect in a VPN, but it has one feature that most VPNs don't have, trustworthiness. The world's third most popular web browser has been testing this for a little over a year now with a browser extension that went by the name Firefox Private Network or Firefox VPN.

Features

  • Mozilla VPN offers a flat monthly subscription with no annual obligation.
  • Covers you on five registered devices.
  • Exclusively uses the WireGuard protocol, which is a bold move considering how relatively new this protocol is in the market.
  • Has servers in 31 countries spread all across the globe, powered by Moldat, another company that's really well known for its security reputation.

Disadvantages

  • Mozilla VPN is very restrictive in their device usage policy.
  • Only has the WireGuard connection protocol.
  • Has limited server options and very few advanced features.

Trustworthiness

Mozilla VPN has a trustworthiness that is unmatched by most VPNs on the market today. They are upfront about their ownership and management, and they are committed to protecting user data.

I'm willing to accept a limited feature set with Mozilla VPN because I trust that they're going to handle my data properly and I anticipate that over time, Mozilla VPN will be adding additional features that will put them more on par with the rest of the competition.

Conclusion

Mozilla VPN may not be the most feature-rich VPN on the market, but its trustworthiness and commitment to user data make it a compelling option for those who value their online security and privacy.
