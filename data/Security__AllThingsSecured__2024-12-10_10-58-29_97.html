Installing a VPN on Your WiFi Router: A Step-by-Step Guide

To secure your internet connection and protect your online identity, installing a VPN on your WiFi router is a crucial step. In this tutorial, we will walk you through the process of setting up a VPN on your router, using ExpressVPN as an example.

Prerequisites

Before we begin, make sure you have a VPN subscription, either through a commercial service like ExpressVPN or by setting up your own VPN server. Also, ensure that your router has integrated VPN functionality or is compatible with VPN configurations.

Step 1: Log into Your Router

Connect your computer to your router via WiFi and open an internet browser. Type in the router's IP address (usually 198.168.1.1) or use the URL address printed on the back or side of your router. This will bring you to the login page, where you will enter your credentials or create an account.

Step 2: Check for VPN Functionality

Navigate to the backend admin panel and look for the VPN tab. If your router has integrated VPN functionality, you should see a VPN tab or an option to add profiles. If not, we will cover alternative options later.

Step 3: Set Up a PPTP Connection

Assuming your router has VPN functionality, navigate to the PPTP settings and create a new connection. Choose a server location (e.g., New York) and configure the settings. You will need to enter the VPN server address, username, and password. For ExpressVPN, you can use the settings provided in the manual configuration section.

Step 4: Set Up an OpenVPN Connection

Go back to the account settings and look for OpenVPN. Download the.ovpn file for the server location you want to connect to (e.g., Chicago). Then, upload the file to the OpenVPN settings and configure the username and password. Ensure the.ovpn file is uploaded correctly, and you should see the connection activate after a minute or so.

Alternative Options

If your router doesn't have VPN functionality, you can consider flashing the router or buying a pre-flashed router with built-in VPN functionality. Flashing a router involves uploading a new operating system, such as Tomato or DD-WRT, which can be done through the router's firmware upgrade option. However, this can be a complex process and may void your router's warranty.

Alternatively, you can purchase a pre-flashed router from a reputable vendor, which will be delivered to your doorstep with the VPN provider pre-installed.

Conclusion

By following these steps, you can ensure that your WiFi router is secure and protected by a VPN. Remember to stay safe online and consider upgrading to a fortified router to keep your connection secure.
